Most Famous Irish Painters

Jack Butler Yeats, 1871 – 1957

Jack B. Yeats was one of the most important Irish painters of the 20th century. He was born on 23rd August 1871 and died on 28th March 1957. He was the son of was a well-known portrait painter John Butler Yeats. He was privately educated in Sligo, Ireland and then attended various art schools in London, Including the Westminster School of Art.

William Orpen, 1871 – 1957

Painter Louis Le Brocquy (Irish, 1916–2012) is best known for his series of portraits of well-known authors and artists. Le Brocquy, a Dublin native, attended Trinity College to study chemistry while working for the family business. Mostly self-taught, he started going to Europe in 1938 to study painting at a number of prominent institutions, including the National Gallery in London and the Louvre in Paris. He returned to Ireland in 1940, when he established the Irish Exhibition of Living Art.

Louis Le Brocquy, 1916 – 2012

Major Sir William Orpen, an Irish artist who mostly produced work in London, lived from 27 November 1878 to 29 September 1931. Sir Orpen was a skilled draftsman as well as a well-liked and financially successful portrait painter for the affluent in Edwardian society, however many of his most striking works are self-portraits.
